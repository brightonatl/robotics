How to Earn Points

  • Complete Lab Bounties: Pick an active task, build it, and show your working project or code to your mentor. The mentor will verify your work and award the points directly!
  • Performance & Behavior: Your ranking can be significantly improved by demonstrating excellent classroom behavior, high performance, and the speed at which you complete lab assignments.
  • Table Competitions: Compete with your peers! Earn extra points by actively participating and dominating in fast-paced class table competitions.

About The Program

Empowering the next generation of innovators, engineers, and leaders at Brighton International School, Raipur.

At Brighton International School, our Robotics Program is designed to ignite curiosity, foster innovation, and prepare students for the future of technology.

Through hands-on learning, students engage with cutting-edge robotics technologies, programming, and engineering concepts at our modern ATL Brighton facility. This workflow allows them to design, assemble, and test their own unique electronic creations. The program heavily emphasizes critical thinking, collaborative teamwork, and creative problem-solving, equipping students with real-world technical skills in STEM.

From foundational coding blocks to complex electronic engineering, our specialized syllabus is built to challenge and inspire pupils. Our program expands past normal school boundaries, pushing students to participate in national and international events, local workshops, and direct guidance with hardware inventors. With access to modern labs and dedicated guidance, students at Brighton are constructing the future today.

The IdeaBox Initiative

Have an idea that could change the world? We have installed physical IdeaBox hubs across the school campus. Students are highly encouraged to pitch their unique, tech-driven solutions designed to solve larger problems in society—from environmental protection to sustainable urban design.

Selected submissions receive special mentorship, lab support, high-end components, and guidance to build functional prototypes for national exhibitions and innovation competitions.
